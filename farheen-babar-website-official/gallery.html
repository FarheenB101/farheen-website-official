Me, the Photo Tour


Click each image for more information!

My main hobby includes drawing professionally. I use IBS Paint digitally but still practice using traditional art.

The media I enjoy and consume play a vital role in shaping my creativity and artistic expression. Selling my art online and managing multiple art accounts has allowed me to share my journey and growth with others. A significant source of my progress stems from Figure Drawing for Artists by Steve Hudson—a book that has guided my techniques and inspired me for years. Following his work has been instrumental in refining my skills and passion for art.


The first picture is a drawing I made of Leon Kennedy from the game Resident Evil 2. Being one of the first art pieces I made digitally, it is rough and includes dark lineart. This was when I was studying face and body proportions in order to improve.

 

The second picture is Miguel from Spider-Man: Across the Spider-Verse. The art in the movie significantly affected my personal art style and made it especially more colorful, with a clear understanding of light and shadow.

I am a member of the Data Enterprise Management Association (DEMA)

As a Presidential Associate member of DEMA, I take pride in assisting the president with key responsibilities, including planning events, securing sponsorships, fostering networking opportunities, and representing the association. This year, I had the privilege of being part of the DEM Soiree, a highlight event where we introduced our club and our esteemed program sponsor professor. Looking ahead, I am thrilled to contribute to the planning of TechTrack 2025, a renowned conference celebrated for its cutting-edge networking and innovative opportunities. It’s exciting to be at the forefront of initiatives that drive meaningful connections and inspire growth!


Attending the Mock Trial club at UTM every semester has been one of the most thrilling and enriching experiences of my university life. It combines my love for critical thinking, public speaking, and a touch of dramatic flair. Every case we analyze feels like stepping into a courtroom drama, where logic and persuasion battle it out. The camaraderie with teammates, the adrenaline of presenting arguments, and the satisfaction of outwitting opposing counsel make it an experience I eagerly look forward to every term. Mock Trial has not just honed my skills but also deepened my passion for legal reasoning and debate!
